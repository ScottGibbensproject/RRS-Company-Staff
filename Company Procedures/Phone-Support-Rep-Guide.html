Company Overview

What RRS is and what we do

What Is Rate Remover Software?

Rate Remover Software (RRS) is a point-of-sale and invoicing platform built specifically for independent auto repair shops. Our software enables compliant dual pricing (also called "cash discount"), which lets shop owners pass card processing costs to customers who pay by card — legally and transparently.

Think of it like gas station pricing: one price for cash, a slightly higher price for card. The software handles all the math automatically.

Dual Pricing Explained

The core concept — know this inside and out

What Is Dual Pricing?

Dual pricing displays two prices for every item:

  • Cash Price — the base price (what the merchant enters)
  • Card Price — base price + non-cash fee (calculated automatically)

If the customer pays cash, they pay the lower price. If they pay by card, the slightly higher price covers the processing cost. The software handles all the math.

Example

CashCard (4% fee)
Repair service$100.00$104.00
Sales tax (8%)$8.00$8.32
Total$108.00$112.32
Key Detail: Sales Tax The non-cash fee applies ONLY to the sale amount, NOT to the sales tax itself. The software handles this automatically. If a merchant asks why tax is slightly different between cash and card — this is why.

Why Not Just "Surcharge"?

AspectSurchargingDual Pricing (What We Do)
DisplaySeparate surcharge line on receiptTwo prices shown for each item
LegalIllegal in NY; restricted elsewhereLegal in all 50 states
ComplianceOften violates Visa/MC rulesCompliant when properly implemented
Customer feelFeels like a penaltyFeels like natural pricing (gas station model)

The Gas Station Comparison

When callers or merchants question dual pricing, use this: "It works just like gas station pricing — one price for cash, one for credit. Customers see and understand both prices upfront. No surprises."

Chatbot Handles: Basic dual pricing explanations, "how does cash discount work?" questions. Escalate to a rep if the caller has compliance-specific or state law questions.

Merchant Workflow

End-to-end process a merchant follows daily
  1. Customer arrives — Merchant creates or selects the customer, enters vehicle info (make/model/year or VIN)
  2. Create Estimate — Look up parts via PartsTech, add labor/misc items. System auto-calculates cash and card prices.
  3. Send for Approval — Send estimate by text or email. Customer clicks Approve or Reject.
  4. Repair Order — Approved estimate becomes a Repair Order (printed for tech, no pricing shown). Tech adds fulfillment notes during work.
  5. Create Invoice — When work is done, Repair Order converts to Invoice. Feedback notes added.
  6. Collect Payment — Customer pays by card (terminal or link), cash, check, or split. Software calculates correct price per method.
  7. Settlement — Card transactions settle daily. Merchant checks Daily Transaction Report to reconcile.
  8. QuickBooks Sync — If connected, invoice auto-syncs to QB Online (one-way only).
Common Call: "My estimate didn't go through" — ask if they sent via text or email, verify the customer's phone/email is correct, and confirm they have a Pro plan (texting requires Pro).

QuickBooks Integration

One-way sync to QuickBooks Online only
Critical Limitations:
  • QuickBooks Online only — Desktop and Self-Employed are NOT supported
  • One-way sync only — RRS pushes to QB. Changes in QB do NOT sync back.
  • All edits must be made in RRS first — QB updates automatically

What Syncs

  • Invoices (when Repair Order becomes Invoice) — including amounts, line items, customers
  • Customers are auto-created in QB
  • Line items are auto-created in QB

What Does NOT Sync

  • Estimates (only invoices)
  • Split payment details (only the total)
  • Vehicle information or technician notes

How Cash/Card Amounts Work in QB

  1. Invoice syncs to QB at the card price initially
  2. If merchant records a cash/check payment in RRS, system sends an adjustment to reduce the QB invoice to the cash amount
  3. If merchant records a card payment, amount stays as-is in QB

Things Merchants Must NOT Do in QB

ActionWhy It's a Problem
Edit invoices in QBBreaks sync — QB version becomes out of sync with RRS
Change invoice amounts in QBRRS doesn't know about the change
Delete invoices in QBCreates residual sync issues
Rename items in QBCreates duplicates on next sync

Troubleshooting Checklist

IssueSolution

Dejavoo Terminals & Hardware

Payment terminals, setup, and troubleshooting

Terminal Setup Requirements

Every merchant needs four items from EPI/Dejavoo to connect their terminal:

ItemFormatWhat It Is
TPN12 digitsTerminal Processing Number — merchant's processing account ID
AuthKey10 chars (case sensitive)Authentication for terminal (card-present) transactions
Ecom AuthKey100-170 chars (case sensitive)Authentication for payment links (card-not-present)
Register ID7 digitsLinks to the specific terminal device
Critical: All dual pricing math happens inside RRS. The terminal receives the final amount only. If the terminal also adds fees, the customer gets double-charged. The Dejavoo terminal should NOT have any surcharge or cash discount program enabled on its side.

Display Name Rules

When creating a merchant account: letters and spaces only. No special characters, no apostrophes, no numbers.

  • "Dan's Auto" must be entered as "Dans Auto"
  • "A&B Repair" must be "A B Repair"

Mobile App

Progressive Web App — not in app stores
Not a Native App: RRS uses a Progressive Web App (PWA) at app.myrateremover.com. It is NOT downloaded from the App Store or Google Play. Merchants add it to their home screen from the browser.

iPhone / iPad Installation

  1. Open app.myrateremover.com in Safari (must be Safari, not Chrome)
  2. Tap the Share button (square with up arrow)
  3. Scroll down, tap "Add to Home Screen"
  4. Name it (e.g., "RRS") and tap Add

Android Installation

  1. Open app.myrateremover.com in Chrome (must be Chrome, not Samsung Internet)
  2. Tap the three-dot menu (top right)
  3. Tap "Install app" or "Add to Home Screen"
  4. Tap Install
