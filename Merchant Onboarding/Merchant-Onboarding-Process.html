How Dual Pricing Works on Your Estimates

When you create an estimate in Rate Remover, you enter your cash price — the base price for parts, labor, and any other charges. The software automatically calculates the card price by adding your configured non-cash rate (typically 4%).

Both prices appear side-by-side on every estimate and invoice your customer sees. There's no separate "surcharge" line — just two clear prices, exactly like a gas station.

Example:

  • Brake job (cash price): $350.00
  • Brake job (card price at 4%): $364.00
  • Customer chooses how to pay — cash gets the lower price, card gets the adjusted price

The non-cash fee applies only to the sale amount, not to sales tax. The software handles this automatically.

Daily Discounting & Deposits

When your customers pay by card, the processing fees are deducted daily from each deposit — not at the end of the month. This is called "daily discounting" and it means:

  • You receive your card payment deposits every business day
  • Each deposit is the net amount (card sales minus processing fees)
  • No surprise monthly fee bill — fees are already accounted for in each deposit

The software's Daily Transaction Report shows you exactly what to expect in each deposit, so reconciling is straightforward.

Common Questions

Is dual pricing legal?
Yes. Dual pricing (showing a cash price and a card price) is legal in all 50 states. It is different from "surcharging," which adds a fee as a separate line item and is restricted in some states. Our software implements true dual pricing, not surcharging.

Will my customers be upset about two prices?
Most shop owners find that customers understand it immediately — it's the same concept as gas station pricing. The vast majority of customers have no issue. Many choose to pay by card anyway because the convenience is worth the small difference.

Can I use the software without a terminal?
Yes. Once your account is active, you can create estimates and invoices and send payment links by text or email. You don't need the physical terminal to get started.

What about PartsTech?
PartsTech integrates directly with Rate Remover for parts lookup, VIN decoding, and labor guides. A free PartsTech account works great — and if you already use PartsTech, it connects right in.

How long does setup take?
Your application is typically approved within 1–2 business days. Your terminal arrives in 5–7 business days. Your onboarding call walks you through everything from start to finish.

Onboarding Scheduling

How and when to book the onboarding call

When to Schedule

The agent should schedule the onboarding call while they are still with the merchant, immediately after the application is submitted. This ensures the merchant has a concrete date and stays engaged through the pre-onboarding period.

Scheduling Timeline

Rule of Thumb: Schedule the onboarding call 6–10 business days after the application date. Terminals typically arrive in 5–7 business days, but allowing a buffer prevents rescheduling if delivery is slightly delayed.

How to Book

  1. Open the GHL booking link for "New Merchant Onboarding"
  2. Select a date 6–10 business days out from the application date
  3. Complete the New Merchant Onboarding form (see below)
  4. Confirm the appointment with the merchant before leaving

Booking Form: "New Merchant Onboarding"

The GHL booking form includes two qualifying questions:

New Merchant Onboarding
Does the merchant have a PartsTech account with at least one supplier account connected?
○ Yes ○ No
If "No" is selected, the booking will be declined.
Do you have a terminal ready to install?
○ Yes ○ No
PartsTech is a hard requirement. If the merchant does not have a PartsTech account with at least one connected supplier, the booking form will decline the appointment. This is why the pre-onboarding emails emphasize PartsTech setup — it must be done before the onboarding call can be scheduled.

What Happens If the Terminal Hasn't Arrived?

The terminal question ("Do you have a terminal ready to install?") is informational, not a blocker. If the terminal hasn't arrived yet:

  • The onboarding call can still proceed — we'll set up the software, connect PartsTech, and configure the account
  • The merchant can start using the software immediately by sending payment links to customers (no terminal needed)
  • Terminal installation can be completed in a brief follow-up call or self-service with our support team
